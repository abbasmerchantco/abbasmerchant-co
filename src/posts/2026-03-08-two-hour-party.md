I picked this up because my Melbourne social circle is smaller than I'd like and "just meet more people" isn't a plan. Gray's insight is that it *can* be a plan — if you treat it as operations rather than vibes.

The system: two hours, hard stop. Weeknight. Name tags (yes, really). Icebreakers on a schedule. Guests told exactly when it ends so saying yes is cheap.

What I love is that every rule exists to lower activation energy — for the host and the guests. It's the same principle as any good maintenance system: reduce the friction of doing the right thing until it happens by default.
