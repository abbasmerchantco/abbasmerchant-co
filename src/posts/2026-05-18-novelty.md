An honest inventory: mechanical trade → oil and gas → wind farms → electricity networks → water. Land Rover → Pajero → Defender → Jeep → sensible work car. Sportbikes → adventure bikes. Each switch felt like growth at the time.

The generous read: range. Every industry taught me something the next one needed, and being the person who's seen five sectors is genuinely rare.

The less generous read: I get bored, and I've built a life that lets me outrun the boredom instead of sitting with it.

I don't think it resolves cleanly. But I've noticed the pattern is only a liability when I abandon things *before the compounding starts*. The trick probably isn't to stop seeking novelty — it's to pick a small number of things that are allowed to compound regardless.
