Third viewing, and it lands differently every time. The first watch it's a puzzle box. The second, a linguistics lecture. The third, you realise the aliens are barely the point.

The question the film actually asks: if you knew exactly how something would end — a relationship, a project, a life — would you still choose it? Louise says yes. Not out of resignation, but because the ending doesn't cancel the middle.

That's a more useful philosophy than most philosophy books I've bounced off. Villeneuve smuggles it in under a first-contact plot, the way the best sci-fi always does.
