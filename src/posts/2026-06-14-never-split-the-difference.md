**Rating: 5/5.** One of maybe three books that changed how I operate day-to-day rather than just how I think.

What actually stuck, months later:

- **Labelling.** "It sounds like you're worried about the timeline" does more work than any counter-argument I've ever made.
- **Calibrated questions.** "How am I supposed to do that?" hands the problem back without saying no.
- **"No" is the start.** People need to feel safe saying no before they'll genuinely say yes.

The tactical stuff (mirroring, the late-night FM DJ voice) is fun, but the durable insight is emotional: people don't agree with arguments, they agree with people who make them feel understood. Which, if you squint, is the same lesson as Carnegie — Voss just proved it works with hostage-takers.
