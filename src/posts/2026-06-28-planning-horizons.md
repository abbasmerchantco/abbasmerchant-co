Every maintenance team I've ever walked into lives about two weeks ahead of the work. Everything past that is fog. And when your horizon is two weeks, everything feels urgent, because everything *is* urgent — you never see anything coming.

The fix isn't working harder inside the fog. It's extending the horizon.

## The mechanics

1. **Freeze a window.** Nothing enters the next two weeks except genuine emergencies. This feels impossible for about a month, then becomes the thing everyone defends.
2. **Plan backwards from constraints.** Access, parts, permits, people — sequence around the scarcest one.
3. **Make the backlog visible.** A backlog you can see shrinks. A backlog in someone's head grows.

When we did this across a distribution network, the planned horizon went from 2 weeks to 10, and the overdue backlog halved. Not because anyone worked harder — because the work stopped ambushing us.

The meta-lesson: most "capacity problems" are visibility problems wearing a disguise.
