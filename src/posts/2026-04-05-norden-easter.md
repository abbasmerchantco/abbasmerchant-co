The plan was simple: four days, mostly gravel, camp light. The Khafra, the quilt, the pad — the whole ultralight kit strapped to the Norden.

Three days of it were as good as riding gets. Empty roads, cold mornings, that specific silence you only get when the engine's off and you're an hour from anyone.

Day four, tired and twenty minutes from done, I dropped the bike at walking pace and my ankle paid the bill. Partial ligament strain, says the GP.

The lesson isn't "don't drop the bike." It's that 90% of my mistakes — riding, work, everything — happen in the last 10% of the effort, when I've mentally already finished. The trip isn't over until the bike's on the stand.

Route notes and photos to come when the ankle lets me sit at a desk properly.
